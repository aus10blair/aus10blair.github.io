The Shade Room

The Shade Room is the first project where I oversaw the User Interface. Along with a very helpful group to collaborate on this application,
I did research on the functionality of my design. During this time period, I got into a car accident which caused a setback on my production and
knowledge obtained. The Shade Room's focus was to develop an Android Studio mobile application that would challenge the user to identify the
square containing the darkest shade of a random color provided. Initially, the gameplay screen was a simple constraint-construction in Android Studio
which turned out to be the best part of my final design. This application sparked my interest in User Interface and front-end development. When I fell
short in performance, designing pages that I and the client felt were subpar to the actual game concept, I decided to improve my knowledge and experience
with User Interface. This project was a learning experience and highlighted my design and development weaknesses. A major weakness was the color scheme because
of my harsh hexadecimal color codes decisions. The vectors of the Home Screen and the High Score screen are not up to standards for a functioning Android application.
Although this app ran smoothly, the color scheme and the vectors are harsh on the eyes. The harshness can be seen in the images of the screens below. Along with this,
